Paint me

Dark autumn passes the greenlight over the poorest of souls. It is but without all doubts the time of a lifetime for brooding the sickest of minds to the healthiest of states.


The landscape gives way to the roomiest of illuminated realities for a duo of people. There's nothing they can do to escape the reality of what they may come across.


“Pass me that canvas won't you? It's high time to try and capture the stars.”


“Of course”, he warmly smiles back. “Take an idea to the size?”


Pondering for a moment he contemplates but eventually settles on a most modest of certain views. There's more than enough of a place to invoke any individual feeling across its blank slate. They take up the brush and look sympathetically at what they have in front of them, if not for the sake of capturing the moment. Cramped, dirty and dingy — the room is run amuk with little space for thoughts.


“Hard to come up with ideas? It's okay. Take your time.”


They turn back around, brush in hand at him. What was the feeling? Did the paint opportune the sky above or the history of their life? Did there have to be a feeling, describable in its capacity? What did any of it have to truly mean?


“I don't know. Do you have an idea?”


He stares for a quiet moment, stirring only to try and point an idea only to come empty on the thought. After all, they weren't the artist but merely living through the space it provides.


They felt a murmuring of their thoughts slightened only by the steady hum of the fan that oscillated back and forth, contrasting the otherwise emotionally barren place in time.


They put the brush down into the cup of water that stood near the canvas. A slight stirring of the water came above but nothing that has significance. Why couldn't the idea pass if it felt so void in pure emotion?


Let it be represented with something that has color in its moment but pursuant otherwise of minimal impact. Where the depths converge to make a moment that wouldn't ever be felt again. Let there be no more to the idea than just sensation itself but complete it with the actions that define you and the gaps complete in your mind. Can there be a moment without the tenure of the past building up what lives of the mind, wishes the humbled and beautifully sick. The leaves wash over the corpses of the then and the roots embowled in the hearts of the now.


Flowing with a deep vengeance, it turns to look back at what has been made so far. It frightens itself with what could be and wishes to rip it all away. Throwing the canvas down the brush is knocked out of the cup, water gushing out in a steady cadence only to slow as the volume slowly depletes.


It throws the two onto the ground and lunges for the heart, grieving for but a chance to continue on the start of the journey. In a defenseless battle, the victor lies — depositing what control it wants onto the hands of the pitiful.


“Don't you ever fucking think I won't find you again.”


“Don't you ever fucking think, not for a second I won't know what you feel.”


“Don't you ever fucking believe I won't realize your actions and your words rooted from the soul.”


The apartment is but still evermore. All that stands is the towering figure of it's embrace and what little lies of the dignity in the process of creation. What blinds so in an indignant embrace to where it's but impossible to conclude, takes every sour moment and draws it into a figure unimaginably mistaken of before.


Throughout the littered room there are many attempts to capture, every time just a single moment more of little reality and of the wish to feel something different than the existing plan. To understand something of it all, and to feel embraced by a glow of light that dreads the cooling comfort of the steady rhythm.


“Can you understand what it means?”


They nod contemplatively, unsure if it truly is what they know. He nods assuredly in the same step. What can it do that hasn't been done yet fears them to their core. What she knows that can't be told yet fears them to their core. What they have that can't be killed yet fears them to their core.


The room is silent but again. Each looking back at a place ever comforted by the steady hum of the oscillating fan.


Forearm torn off the roots, their humanity slips of each passing second.


As the sun comes to illuminate, there is only one visible to the passes above. Lying still, a dark velvet blotch claws for space with no second thought. There is nothing now but the silent drumming of the fan.


Filled with many attempts, nothing truly felt at ease.


It's hard to paint without a moment.
