Tornages.

How long ago was it? 40, 20, no, no. It was, roughly 12 years ago, that I found the secret lair hidden under grandpa’s musty, moist and humid vegetable garden. As I walked around, I felt my sandals hitting the rocky path, mossy and wet to the touch, curve them making me feel unstable while balancing on them. I was pondering whether to stop and stare at the blossoming flowers or sit under the tree. I decided to sit under the tree, the damp grass wettening my dress and the sunlight-

“Wake up, Subject: 44. Your memories are not real.”

“Wha-t? Hu-hh, w-ait I’m n-o-t a gi-rl.”

Shivering from the cold of the room, I rubbed my hands to generate some heat.

The man standing across the room gave a cold hard stare, beaming me down with no emotion in his eyes. I could feel his stone-hard gaze, washing over me.

The man replied, 

“You, you were placed here for a reason.” 

“So, i-is i-t poss-i-bl-e, to m-ayb-e ra-i-se the h-ea-t?”

“Erm, sure, if it helps you snap out of that.”

The man walked over to a screen and gestured his hands like he was turning a knob, his hands suddenly were grasping a real knob, and as he retreated, the knob disappeared. 

“Alright, 44. You are the only one who recovered. You are strong enough to save the world from it’s torn state.”

“Me? I mean, what is my name?”

“Hrm, let’s see, I guess Anthony will do.”

I suddenly felt myself changing, and I was fit with clothing that, I have never seen before. A green slightly ripped hoodie grasped around my chest and wrapped around my arms, cascading itself around, and tightened itself to fit me. Jeans began adjusting themselves for my body, and I felt myself getting lighter. The liquid around me faded and I was placed on the floor, dripping wet.

“Oh, right.”

“fHUAWugh, aGH!!”

A burst of hot air came out from the walls, drying me off instantly.

“Welcome to the team, Anthony.”
