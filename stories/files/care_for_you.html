Care for you

I let the shower cry for me because I don’t have many tears left to give. Water pouring down my neck and across my face, I wonder whether this is the true melody of emotion or a simple facade I put on — the suds streaking away from my body as steam rises from the shower head. 

    

It’s difficult to truly put the days and nights into words, each fleeting moment being characterized by what-ifs or what-could-be’s. I wonder still whether any of it truly was something of note or just another second of desperate clinging to the past and its comforting ignorance. I turn contemplatively at the light near my desk, cluttered papers and imaginations galore; almost jeering at me for leaving past gones present. I scrape the chair against the ground like a hopeless addict, bringing it close to one of the few visible windows. Pushing away the drapes, I unveil the stage of a wonderful symphony of sights and colors. I feel washed with the profound nature of a place so surreal and yet all so familiar. 


Angst couldn’t possibly lie in a reality with such great depth and piercing beauty. I give a few beats to admire the small glimmer of reality situated out the backyard of my home, to only get a sense of truth from a familiar tone. Getting up, I make my way over towards my dining room and I pick up my phone-line — untangling the cord as I listen carefully to the spoken cadence. Spoken tongue that I recall but can’t seem to fit in the volume of my unthinking mind. It reminds me of what niche I fill in the pocket of this environment, the small shimmer of fool’s gold I love to chase terminally. Nodding and responding half-heartedly, time slowly shoots by as I ready myself to step out the door, ticking uncomfortably in a manner I never wished for. I only hang up the phone when the moment dies, starting to walk towards my bedroom illuminated by a dull warm glow. Brushing my hair and deodorizing, I stare at a familiar face husking a personality trying to wade the rough tides of guilt and denial. The clock continues to tick, marching along the chorus of a second.


I wallow in fear of letting it take hold, for I’d live to trust it all again. The whir of my bikes’ gears, and the motion of my pedaling map the route I follow – helmet pressed against my head with a deep embrace I yearn to never let go. I wonder if I hold tight enough, that I’ll keep all my feelings bottled in a place that knows me best and’ll never act out.
